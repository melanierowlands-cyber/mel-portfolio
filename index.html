in the studio
the short version

I've had the kind of career that gives you range — and I've leaned into it.

I started my working life as a Junior Analyst at Goldman Sachs in London — a useful apprenticeship in doing careful, rigorous work under pressure. I came home to Cape Town and co-founded Fineday Design Studio, where I spent seven years building food brands and packaging from scratch — work that went on to include two licensed international pattern collaborations (Studio E Fabrics and Nested Bean, both in the US).

After that I shifted closer to the technical side as a Junior Data Analyst, then back to building something of my own. Today I run iNGENiO — a full-stack digital art learning platform for children — where I own the platform architecture, the LMS, the UX, and the brand. Every pixel and every line of code.

What all of that adds up to is a genuinely unusual combination: design instinct, e-commerce fluency, platform & LMS development, and a growing practice in AI-enabled tools. It's the kind of toolkit that suits small and mid-sized businesses that don't need an agency — they need a trusted pair of hands.
